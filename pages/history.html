The Origin Story

Built on a Conviction, Not a Commission

Stackly was not born from a corporate spin-off or a private equity roll-up. It was founded by a single advisor who believed the financial industry had a structural conflict problem — and decided to build something different.

Sowat Ahsan spent a decade inside a bulge-bracket brokerage watching clients receive advice shaped by product quotas rather than portfolio needs. In 2010, he left to establish a firm where the only metric that mattered was client outcome. The first three clients were family offices who followed him out of the old firm. Within five years, Stackly had grown entirely through referrals — no advertising, no cold outreach, no sales team.

That founding philosophy — that trust is earned through results, not marketing — remains the operating principle of every engagement we take on today.

Strategic Turning Points

The Decisions That Defined Us

Several deliberate strategic choices separated Stackly from the broader advisory market. These were not easy decisions — but they were the right ones.

Going Fee-Only in 2015

When Stackly converted to a pure fee-only RIA structure in 2015, we lost three large institutional accounts that preferred the old commission model. We gained twelve new family office clients within the following year who specifically sought a conflict-free advisor. It was the most consequential business decision in our history.

Opening London in 2016

Expanding internationally was not part of the original plan. A cluster of U.S. clients with significant European holdings needed cross-border tax coordination that no single-jurisdiction firm could provide. We opened the London desk to serve them — and it became our fastest-growing office within three years.

Building Proprietary Risk Models in 2022

Rather than licensing third-party risk software, Stackly invested three years and a dedicated quant team to build proprietary stress-testing models calibrated to our specific client profiles. The result: a drawdown isolation framework that outperformed industry benchmarks during the 2022 rate-shock cycle by an average of 6.4%.

Capping Client Intake in 2023

In 2023, Stackly made the unusual decision to cap new client intake at 20 engagements per year. The reason was simple: we refused to dilute the quality of senior partner attention each client receives. A waiting list now exists for new advisory relationships — a constraint we consider a mark of quality, not a limitation.
